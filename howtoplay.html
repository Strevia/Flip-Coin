How To Play

Q: What do I do?
Flip a coin. In general, it should be obvious the next thing to do; however, if you are still confused, continue!
Q: What are sides?
Sides allow you to buy robots.
Q: What are robots?
Each robot flips 1 coin every second. Since all of them do it the same, each will land on the same side.
Q: What are builders?
Builders, to start, will each build one robot. If all of them cannot build, no robots will be built.
Q: What is intelligence?
Intelligence is gained after you reach 100 robots. Intelligence is logarithmic, meaning each intelligence is 10x as hard to get as the last. However, they boost robots and builders.
Q: What is a revolution?
Revolutions have a chance of happening after you get intelligence. The chance of a revolution increases with more intelligence. During a revolution, robots and builders refuse to flip coins and build more robots. However, robots will generate 100 times as much intelligence, and log2(builders) each get 1 Art.
Q: What is art?
Art on its own does not do anything, however at certain intervals (4, 9, 16, 25, etc.) they will make an artwork.
Q: What is artwork?
Artwork has multiple uses. The first being that each artwork doubles the amount of intelligence you get. The second is that you can sell it for money in the market tab.
Q: Infinity?!
In Javascript floats can only store values up to 2^1024, or around 100CM in in-game notation. Because of this, Infinity is reached by this game. If something reaches Infinity, it means that you don't have to worry about it anymore.
Q: What is the market?
The market is where you sell your artwork. You can sell your artwork to get money. The selling price for artwork fluctuates between 0.5x normal price and 1.5x normal price.
Q: What is creativity?
Each artwork sold grants 1 creativity. The base price of artwork sold is log2(creativity), or 1, whichever is bigger.
Q: What is money?
Money is gained by selling artwork, and is used to buy books.
Q: What are books?
Books are made with money, and allow you to keep intelligence when you singularity.
Q: What is a singularity?
Singularities are this game's prestige. When you singularity, you must destroy all of your robots, builders, heads, tails, and sides, as well as lose most of your intelligence. However, each book provides you with an additional intelligence. Also, the price of books reset when you singularity. Every 10 creativity allows you to make one enlightened robot, multiplying art production and allowing you to make 1 more art per revolution.
